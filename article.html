6 Fun Facts About Snails

From mother of pearl to mythology, there's more to snails than you thought

By Caitlyn Cole

Snails: some see them as pests, others as pets, and still others as a source of food. There are land snails, freshwater snails, and sea snails. They symbolize patience, but also laziness. One thingâ€™s for sure, though: snails are pretty fascinating creatures. Here are six fun facts you might not have known about snails.

Snail Fact 1

Some sea snails have been used to make jewelry and buttons, as their shells are a source of mother of pearl. Hermit crabs also sometimes use the shells of sea snails as protection.

Snail Fact 2

Not only are snails a popular food dish in many countries around the world, snail eggs are sometimes sold as snail caviar.

Snail Fact 3

A land snail's slime enables it to climb vertically without falling. It is also widely used as a skincare ingredient.

Snail Fact 4

Snails in the Muricidae family produce a secretion that works as a purple dye; this was known as Tyrian Purple and was used for expensive garments in ancient times.

Snail Fact 5

A Javanese folklore tale called Keong Emas, meaning Golden Snail, tells about a princess who is transformed into a golden snail by a god to save her from a kidnapper. She is then found and kept as a pet by an old widow, who eventually figures out how to break the spell.

Snail Fact 6

It is thought that the ancient myth of Cupid and his arrows could have been based on the "love darts" that certain snail species use during courtship.
